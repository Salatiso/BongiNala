About BongiNala

BongiNala is more than just a trucking company; it's a legacy built on the foundations of family, dedication, and a profound understanding of the African landscape. Named in honor of his children, Bonga and Nala, our founder, a proud and strong Zulu father, established BongiNala with a vision to connect and empower businesses across Southern Africa.

Though an accomplished academic and COO in a major South African corporate, his passion for logistics and enterprise development led to the birth of BongiNala. From humble beginnings in Newcastle, we have grown into a formidable force in the transport sector, serving esteemed clients like Cashbuild and Builders Warehouse, carrying essential goods such as steel and cement across the nation and into neighboring sub-Saharan countries.

"We believe in building bridges – not just with roads and trucks, but with trust, reliability, and a commitment to the communities we serve. BongiNala is driven by the spirit of 'Ubuntu' – I am because we are."

Our roots in Johannesburg provide a strategic hub, while our heart remains in delivering excellence on every kilometer travelled. We are committed to upholding the highest standards of service, safety, and efficiency, ensuring your cargo reaches its destination securely and on time.

Trucking Resources Hub

Key legal & operational requirements for trucking in Southern Africa. This information is for general guidance only.

South Africa

Driver Requirements:

  • Valid PrDP (Professional Driving Permit) for the correct vehicle category.
  • Minimum age requirements (typically 25 for heavy commercial vehicles).
  • Medical fitness certificate.
  • Sober habits, criminal record check may be required.

Load Requirements:

  • Adherence to National Road Traffic Act for vehicle dimensions and mass.
  • Proper load securing techniques (SANS codes).
  • Permits for abnormal loads if applicable.
  • Regulations for dangerous goods (Hazchem).

Inspections & Licenses:

  • Certificate of Fitness (COF) for commercial vehicles, renewed regularly.
  • Company Operator Card.
  • Vehicle licenses and registration.

Cross-Border:

  • Cross-Border Road Transport Agency (C-BRTA) permits.
  • Customs documentation (SAD 500, manifests).
  • TIP (Temporary Import Permit) for foreign registered vehicles.

Insurance & Medical:

  • Compulsory third-party insurance.
  • Goods-in-Transit (GIT) insurance highly recommended.
  • Driver medical aid/workmen's compensation.

Botswana

Driver Requirements:

  • Valid driver's license equivalent to Botswana Class EC.
  • Professional Driving Permit (PrDP) or equivalent.
  • Medical fitness.

Load Requirements:

  • Compliance with Road Traffic Act and vehicle dimension/weight limits.
  • Secure loading practices.
  • Permits for abnormal or hazardous loads.

Inspections & Licenses:

  • Vehicle roadworthiness certificate.
  • Transport permits from the Department of Road Transport and Safety.

Cross-Border:

  • Bilateral/SADC road transport agreements.
  • Customs clearance (Botswana Unified Revenue Service - BURS).
  • Temporary Import Permits (TIPs) or Carnets.

Insurance & Medical:

  • Motor vehicle third-party insurance (Botswana-specific may be required).
  • GIT insurance.

Zimbabwe

Driver Requirements:

  • Valid driver's license (Class 2 for heavy vehicles).
  • Defensive Driving Certificate often required.
  • Medical examination certificate.

Load Requirements:

  • Adherence to vehicle axle load limits and dimensions.
  • Securement of cargo as per regulations.
  • Permits for abnormal loads from Ministry of Transport.

Inspections & Licenses:

  • Certificate of Fitness (COF) from Vehicle Inspection Department (VID).
  • Operator's License.

Cross-Border:

  • COMESA/SADC permits and protocols.
  • Customs documentation (ZIMRA).
  • Carbon Tax, Road Access Fees.
  • CVG (Commercial Vehicle Guarantee) may be required.

Insurance & Medical:

  • Statutory motor insurance.
  • GIT insurance.

Mozambique

Driver Requirements:

  • Valid Mozambican or SADC-recognized driver's license for heavy vehicles.
  • Professional driving qualifications may be scrutinized.

Load Requirements:

  • Compliance with Mozambican road traffic code (Código de Estrada) on weight/dimensions.
  • Secure loading. Specific permits for oversized cargo.

Inspections & Licenses:

  • Vehicle inspection certificate (Inspeção Periódica Obrigatória).
  • Transport license ("Alvará de Transporte").

Cross-Border:

  • Bilateral agreements, SADC protocols.
  • Customs clearance (Autoridade Tributária de Moçambique).
  • Third-party insurance (often local required). Various road tolls and levies.
  • Port health clearances may apply.

Insurance & Medical:

  • Mandatory third-party liability insurance (Mozambican).
  • GIT insurance.
